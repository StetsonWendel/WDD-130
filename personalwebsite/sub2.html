The Qur'an

The word Qur'an means "recitation." The Qur'an is a recitation or pronouncement of Allah's word. About the size of the New Testament, it is very unlike the New Testament. It is not a book of history and does not tell a story, though there are several narratives in it. Instead it is a collection of individual pronouncements in the words of Allah. Having 114 chapters each is an individual revelation. Muhammad does not speak in the Qur'an nor does a narrator the only speaker is Allah. The Qur'an for Muslims is God's Word, character, attributes, and person revealed in words in a book. This is why many Muslims argue that the Qur'an cannot be translated because no matter how faithfully the translation once changed into different words it is no longer Gods words. This leads some renderings in other languages will adopt carefully worded titles that are meant to remind the reader that what they have is not the Qur'an but an translation of it.
