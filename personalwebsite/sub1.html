History of Islam

The History of Islam starts with the life of Muhammad. Muhammad was born in the Arabian city of Mecca around 570 CE with his prophetic call coming in about 610 CE. Arabian society was polytheistic, and Mecca was a religious center. In the city their was (and still is) a large cubical structure called the Kaaba witched housed the images of the city's Gods. Muslims believe that te Kaaba was built by Abraham and his son Ishmael or repaired by them and built earlier by Adam.
In Muhammad's time annual pilgrimages to the Kaaba to worship Mecca's gods brought visitors and their money to the city. After Muhammad received and then embraced his calling from God, Muhammad started to gather converts who believed his message. Over the course of time a sizable body of Muslims was found in Mecca. This new religion was a problem for the leaders of Mecca as Muhammad's revelations were the words of one god, Allah, and they condemned the existence of any other god. This was an economic threat that if spread would reduce the income from pilgrimages to worship the various deities. This caused leaders to persecute the growing Muslim community making it necessary for them to leave.
Upon invitation Muhammad and the Muslims emigrated to the city of Yathrib. The city would latter be known as Medina. This marked a turning point in Islam as it created the first Islamic state where Muhammad was not just the the religious leader but also the political leader of Medina and a place where Muslims could worship safely.
Continued hostility and opposition from the residents of Mecca led to combat and after eight years of conflict Muhammad and his followers took control of Mecca. They cleansed the Kaaba and rededicating it to the worship of Allah. Making Mecca the most holy city in the religion of Islam.
